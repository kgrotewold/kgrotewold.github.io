Kimberly S. Grotewold, Ph.D.

AI Librarian, Education Specialist, and Learning Designer

I am a tenacious learning technologies researcher, instructional designer, and educator with 18 years of experience across higher education, K-12, and public libraries. My work centers on the intentional, ethical, and equitable integration of emerging tools—particularly artificial intelligence—into teaching and curriculum design.

Having earned my Ph.D. in Learning Technologies from the University of North Texas, I developed the ECET AI Framework, a decision-support tool to help educators evaluate AI products. My scholarship on AI adoption, instructional design, and open educational resources has been published in leading venues, including the Journal of Interactive Learning Research, the Decision Sciences Journal of Innovative Education, and the International Journal of Designs for Learning.

>

Currently, as the AI Librarian and Library Innovation Lab Supervisor at Texas A&M University-San Antonio, I champion generative AI awareness and faculty development. Previously, as Chair of the Texas A&M University System’s CATIE, I coordinated digital learning and accessibility initiatives across 11 institutions and 8 agencies. My passions and strengths lie at the intersection of applied research, learning experience design, and higher education leadership.
